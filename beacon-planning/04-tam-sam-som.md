# Beacon — Market Sizing (TAM / SAM / SOM)

## Summary

| Market | Size | Value |
|---|---|---|
| TAM | All US licensed insurance agents | ~$960M/year |
| SAM | Independent P&C agencies (addressable now) | ~$126M/year |
| SOM Year 1 | Realistic capture | ~$307K ARR |
| SOM Year 2 | Growth trajectory | ~$1.1M ARR |
| SOM Year 3 | Scaled position | ~$2.5M ARR |

---

## TAM — Total Addressable Market

**Who:** Every licensed insurance agent in the United States who communicates with clients.

**Numbers:**
- 400,000+ licensed insurance agents in the US (Bureau of Labor Statistics, IIABA data)
- Every single one of these agents needs to communicate with clients before renewals, after rate increases, and at key lifecycle moments

**Pricing assumption:** $200/month blended average across all potential users

**TAM calculation:**
400,000 agents × $200/month × 12 months = **$960,000,000/year (~$960M)**

**Global expansion (not in scope for launch but relevant for exit conversations):**
- Canada: ~120,000 licensed agents
- UK: ~150,000 licensed advisors/brokers
- Australia: ~50,000
- Global TAM: 2-3x US TAM = **$2B-3B/year**

## SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market

**Who:** Independent P&C (Property & Casualty) agents and agencies in the US. Not captive agents (State Farm, Allstate captives have their carrier's tools). Not health-only agents (different workflow). Not large enterprise brokerages (different buying process, different tools).

**Why this segment:**
- Independent agents control their own software decisions — they can buy Beacon without corporate approval
- P&C agents have the highest renewal volume and retention pressure (homeowner, auto, commercial renewals are annual events that create the exact communication problem Beacon solves)
- This segment is tech-underserved — captive agents get carrier-provided tools, large brokerages get enterprise platforms. Independent agents get nothing built for them.

**Numbers:**
- ~42,000 independent insurance agencies in the US (IIABA data)
- Average agency size: 1-8 agents
- Buying unit: the agency (one subscription per agency, not per agent)

**Pricing assumption:** $250/month blended average per agency
(40% Solo at $149 + 45% Agency at $299 + 15% Office at $499 = $269 blended; rounded to $250 conservatively)

**SAM calculation:**
42,000 agencies × $250/month × 12 months = **$126,000,000/year (~$126M)**

## SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market

**Who:** The subset of the SAM Beacon can realistically capture given its GTM strategy, resources, and competitive position.

**GTM channels and their realistic reach:**
- IIABA Big "I" community: 25,000 member agencies. Realistic penetration Year 1: 0.3% = 75 agencies
- Insurance Journal forums: 50,000+ active readers. Realistic conversion: 0.05% = 25 agencies
- LinkedIn outreach (Independent Insurance Agent titles): millions of profiles, realistic response rate: 0.1% of outreach = 10 agencies
- Referral/word of mouth: 1 agent tells 1 agent. With 30%+ referral rate, every 3 customers generates 1 more
- Agency networks (Smart Choice, SIAA, Keystone): one network endorsement = access to 300-500 agencies

**Assumptions:**
- 5% monthly churn (conservative for this buyer — agents are sticky once they import client data)
- No paid acquisition spend in Year 1 (founder-led, community-driven)

**SOM by year:**

**Year 1 (Months 1-12):**
- Target: 85 paying agencies by month 12
- Mix: 34 Solo ($149) + 38 Agency ($299) + 13 Office ($499)
- MRR at month 12: $5,066 + $11,362 + $6,487 = $22,915
- ARR at month 12: **$274,980 (~$275K)**
- Market share of SAM: 0.2%

**Year 2 (Months 13-24):**
- Target: 300 paying agencies by month 24
- Growth driver: referrals compound, first agency network partnership
- MRR at month 24: ~$80,700
- ARR at month 24: **~$968,000 (~$1M)**
- Market share of SAM: 0.7%

**Year 3 (Months 25-36):**
- Target: 700 paying agencies by month 36
- Growth driver: second network partnership, LinkedIn content flywheel, press coverage
- MRR at month 36: ~$188,300
- ARR at month 36: **~$2,260,000 (~$2.3M)**
- Market share of SAM: 1.7%

**Year 5 (for exit conversation):**
- Target: 2,000 agencies
- ARR: **~$6.5M**
- Market share of SAM: ~4.8%
- Strategic acquirer interest threshold: typically $5-10M ARR for this category

## Why This Market Is Winnable

1. **No dominant AI-native competitor.** The space has legacy tools (Zywave at $2,400-40,000/year) and generic AI (ChatGPT at $20/month). No purpose-built, price-accessible, insurance-specific AI communication tool exists.

2. **High switching cost once adopted.** The E&O log, client data, and voice learning all live in Beacon. Switching means losing your documentation history and retraining a new AI on your style.

3. **Referral-driven market.** Insurance agents trust other agents. One happy agent in an IIABA chapter meeting is worth 10 LinkedIn ads.

4. **Regulatory tailwind.** E&O carriers are increasingly asking for documentation of client communications. Beacon provides this automatically. Over time, this may become a compliance requirement, making Beacon non-optional.

5. **Exit math is clear.** At $5M ARR, Beacon is worth $25-50M to a strategic acquirer (Applied Systems, Vertafore, Zywave, AgentSync). These companies have done this math. Zywave alone has raised $200M+ and has shown appetite for acquisitions.
