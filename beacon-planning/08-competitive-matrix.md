## Competitor Deep Dives

### Zywave

**What they do well:**
- Largest content library in the industry (HR, compliance, insurance content)
- Strong carrier and AMS integrations
- Enterprise credibility — large agencies trust the brand
- 7,000+ agency customers

**What they do poorly:**
- Pricing ($2,400-40,000/year) locks out solo agents and small agencies
- Generic content — same newsletter goes to every client, not personalized
- Notoriously difficult to onboard (3+ months to full adoption, frequently fails)
- Not AI-native — content library was built pre-AI and AI was bolted on
- User reviews on G2: "complicated," "hard to learn," "most producers never use it"
- Not built for solo agents

**Beacon's position vs. Zywave:**
Beacon is not trying to replace Zywave for Sandy's 20-agent firm. Beacon owns the market Zywave can't serve: solo agents and small agencies who need something that works on day one, costs under $500/month, and generates letters that sound personal — not corporate.

When Beacon is large enough to acquire, Zywave is one of the logical buyers.

---

### AgencyRevolution

**What they do well:**
- Marketing automation designed specifically for insurance agencies
- Good email drip campaign functionality
- Integrates with several AMS platforms
- Established player with strong support

**What they do poorly:**
- Drip sequences send the same content to everyone — not personalized
- Can't generate a specific letter for a specific client situation in real time
- Priced for agencies with budget and tech-savvy staff
- No AI-driven content generation
- Focused on campaign marketing, not one-to-one client communication

**Beacon's position vs. AgencyRevolution:**
AgencyRevolution helps agencies send campaigns to their whole book. Beacon helps agents write to a specific client about their specific situation. These are different jobs-to-be-done. An agency could use both — but if forced to choose, Beacon wins on the highest-value use case: the individual retention conversation.

---

### ChatGPT / Claude Direct

**What they do well:**
- Cheap ($20/month)
- Flexible — can write anything
- Agents who are tech-forward already use these
- Good writing quality

**What they do poorly:**
- Not insurance-specific — doesn't know P&C terminology, doesn't understand surplus lines, gets coverage descriptions wrong
- No E&O documentation log — there's no record of what was generated
- No client data management — agent types in everything manually every time
- No Gmail/Outlook integration — agent has to copy-paste
- No renewal calendar — no proactive trigger to use the tool
- No voice learning — starts from scratch every session
- Requires the agent to know how to prompt correctly — most don't

**Beacon's position vs. ChatGPT/Claude:**
ChatGPT is what agents use before they find Beacon. The agent discovers Beacon when they realize ChatGPT generates letters that need heavy editing, doesn't know their clients, doesn't have their E&O log, and requires them to prompt it correctly every single time. Beacon is the purpose-built version of what agents are trying to do with ChatGPT.

Jake (our young producer persona) is the exact customer who uses ChatGPT today and would immediately switch to Beacon.

---

### AMS Built-in Features (EZLynx, AMS360, Applied Epic)

**What they do well:**
- Agents are already in the AMS every day — no new tab required
- Client data is already there — no import needed
- Basic communication templates exist in some platforms
- Deep policy and renewal data integration

**What they do poorly:**
- Communication features are an afterthought, not a priority
- No AI-generated content — templates only
- Templates are generic and not personalized per client situation
- No voice learning, no E&O log specifically for communications
- AMS vendors are not moving fast on AI (Applied Epic has 6,000 known issues; EZLynx AI is basic)
- No Gmail/Outlook OAuth send — still manual copy-paste in most systems

**Beacon's position vs. AMS:**
Agents will always live in their AMS for policy management. Beacon doesn't compete with that. Beacon competes with the communication gap that AMS systems leave wide open. The long-term play is a Beacon → AMS integration (data flows from EZLynx into Beacon automatically), eliminating the CSV import step and making Beacon even stickier.

---

### Status Quo — Word Templates / Doing Nothing

**What they do well:**
- Free
- Familiar
- Agents control the output completely

**What they do poorly:**
- Takes 20-45 minutes per letter
- Results in most letters never being written (it's too slow)
- No E&O documentation
- No centralized history of what was sent
- Templates feel impersonal — clients can tell
- Scales to zero — no agent can write 40 personalized letters per month this way

**Beacon's position vs. doing nothing:**
This is the biggest competitor. Most agents are not using any tool for proactive communication — they're just not doing it. Every agent who understands the retention math ($1,500 average annual premium × losing 14 clients/year = $21,000 in lost annual revenue) will pay $149/month to stop losing clients.

The status quo demo: ask the agent how much time they spend writing renewal letters. Then show Beacon generating one in 60 seconds. The status quo is eliminated in that moment.

## Beacon's Defensible Advantages

### 1. Insurance Specificity (Immediate Advantage)
Beacon knows P&C insurance. The AI has been trained with insurance-specific prompts. It knows what a rate increase explanation letter needs to say, what an E&O-safe disclaimer looks like, what tone to use with a client who's been with the agent for 8 years. Generic AI tools don't have this out of the box.

### 2. Voice Learning (Time-Based Moat)
At MVP, Beacon captures the agent's tone preference (formal / in between / conversational) and sign-off phrase, applying both to every generated letter. In Phase 2, Beacon adds edit-diff learning: every edit the agent makes to a generated letter trains a model of their specific communication style — sentence length, formality signals, common substitutions. After 100+ letters, Beacon writes letters that sound like the agent wrote them personally. This advantage is impossible to replicate quickly. A competitor would need to give the agent time to train their model from scratch.

### 3. E&O Documentation Log (Switching Cost)
An agent's E&O log is a legal record. Once it lives in Beacon — with 18 months of timestamped, client-linked communications — the agent cannot switch tools without losing that history. E&O carriers may eventually require this documentation. Agents will not give it up.

### 4. Gmail/Outlook Integration (Workflow Lock-in)
Once an agent sends letters from Beacon directly through their own Gmail or Outlook, they won't go back to copy-pasting. The send integration is deeply embedded in their daily workflow. Any alternative tool requires them to rebuild that habit.

### 5. Community Trust (Distribution Moat)
Insurance agents trust other agents. Beacon earns trust through IIABA chapters and Insurance Journal forums — not LinkedIn ads. Once the community endorses Beacon, competitors can't buy their way in with ad spend. This distribution moat compounds over time.
