# Beacon — Go-to-Market Plan

## GTM Philosophy

Founder-led and community-first. No paid advertising until organic channels are proven. The insurance agent community is tight-knit and trust-based — one respected agent's recommendation in an IIABA chapter meeting is worth more than $10,000 in LinkedIn ads.

CAC target: <$150. Community strategy keeps this achievable with zero ad spend.

---

## Primary Channels (Priority Order)

### Channel 1: IIABA Big "I" Community — Highest Priority

**Why this first:** 25,000 member agencies. Trust-based community. Agents who attend chapter meetings are exactly the buyer profile — invested in their practice, not passive agents.

**Entry points:**
- IIABA has state chapters that meet quarterly or monthly — request 10-minute presentation slots
- IIABA Big "I" online communities and forums — participate with value-first content, not promotions
- Agents Council for Technology (ACT) — the IIABA arm specifically focused on technology adoption; the right audience for a tech product

**Month 3-6 tactics:**
- Contact 5 state chapter directors with a request to present a 10-minute demo at the next meeting (see outreach script below)
- Post in IIABA communities: retention tips, E&O documentation guidance — earn credibility before selling
- Track which states have the most engaged community activity and prioritize those first

**Realistic outcomes:** Chapter presentation → 20-50 agents in the room → 5-10 trial signups → 1-2 paid conversions per event. At 4 events in Months 3-6: 4-8 new customers directly attributable.

---

### Channel 2: Insurance Journal

**Why:** 50,000+ active readers. Editorial credibility that ad spend cannot replicate. An Insurance Journal byline positions Beacon's founder as an expert, not a vendor.

**Tactics:**
- Pitch one byline article per quarter — written from an agent-benefit perspective, not a product announcement
- Article angle examples:
  - "Why independent agents lose 14 clients a year — and the one habit that stops it"
  - "E&O documentation for client communications: what your carrier actually wants to see"
  - "I tested AI letter tools for insurance agents. Here's what worked and what didn't." (honest comparison, Beacon wins on specifics)
- Include a brief author bio with beacon.get-monolith.com
- Participate in Insurance Journal forums and comment sections with substantive answers — not self-promotion

**Realistic outcomes:** 1 article → 200-500 profile visits → 10-25 trial signups → 2-5 paid conversions.

---

### Channel 3: LinkedIn (Organic, No Paid)

**Why:** The largest professional network with the most granular targeting for "independent insurance agent." Organic content compounds — a post that gets traction surfaces to followers of followers.

**Content rhythm:** 3 posts per week
- Monday: Educational — retention tip, E&O guidance, market insight
- Wednesday: Social proof — agent result, letter example (with permission), before/after workflow
- Friday: Behind-the-scenes / product — feature highlight, honest update, what's being built

**Content that works for this audience:**
- "Here's the renewal letter template I wish I'd had for the rate increase conversation" (actual letter, not a pitch)
- "I asked 10 independent agents what they do about client communication before renewals. The answers were depressing." (honest, relatable)
- "This agent sent 28 renewal letters in her first month with Beacon. She retained 3 clients who had quotes in hand from competitors." (results, with permission)

**DM strategy:** When agents post about client communication frustrations, rate increase challenges, or retention — reply substantively, then DM an offer for a free trial. Do not cold-DM without context.

**Target accounts to engage:**
- Independent agent LinkedIn influencers (1K+ followers in the niche)
- IIABA state association accounts
- Insurance Journal LinkedIn page

**Realistic outcomes:** Posting rhythm established → 200-400 followers by Month 6 → consistent 5-10 trial signups/week from LinkedIn by Month 9.

---

### Channel 4: Referral Program (Month 3 Launch)

**Why:** Insurance agents talk to other agents constantly — study groups, chapter meetings, carrier events. One agent referring Beacon at a study group meeting is the highest-quality lead source possible.

**Mechanics:**
- Every paid account gets a personal referral link (unique URL)
- Referring agent gets 1 free month for every referral who upgrades to paid
- Referred agent gets 1 free month added to their trial (20 letters instead of 10)
- Referral link is accessible from the dashboard — not buried in settings

**Referral ask (when to ask):**
- In the welcome email: "If another agent asks what you're using for renewal letters, tell them." (low-pressure, early)
- At Month 1: when the agent has generated 10+ letters and is clearly getting value — ask directly in an in-app nudge
- At renewal: "You've been with us a year. If you know one agent who should be using Beacon, here's your referral link."

**Target:** 30% of new customers from referrals by Month 12.

---

### Channel 5: Agency Networks (Month 6+ Outreach)

**Why:** Smart Choice, SIAA, Keystone, Orchid, and other independent agency networks each represent 300-1,500 member agencies. One network endorsement bypasses cold outreach entirely and delivers a pre-qualified, warm pipeline.

**The pitch to network directors:**
- Member benefit: free trial for all member agencies
- Revenue share: 10% of first-year revenue from network members who pay
- Value to the network: a member benefit that directly addresses the retention problem their members face

**Approach:**
- Identify network operations directors via LinkedIn
- Warm introduction preferred — IIABA connections may bridge to network contacts
- Demo the product over Zoom before asking for partnership terms

**Timing:** Begin outreach in Month 4, close first partnership Month 6-9.

**One network partnership = 30-50 paying agencies.** The economics of a single network deal justify spending significant founder time on this.

## Phase Timeline

### Pre-Launch: Months 1-2 — Build + Recruit Beta

**Goal:** 5-10 beta agents. Zero paying customers.

**Actions:**
- Post in IIABA online communities: "Building an AI letter tool for independent P&C agents — looking for 5 agents to test it free and give feedback. DM me."
- DM 20 first-degree LinkedIn connections who are independent agents
- Reach out to any personal contacts in insurance
- Do NOT make any public announcement — beta is invitation-only

**Beta criteria for selection:**
- Licensed P&C independent agent (not captive)
- At least 100 active clients
- Genuine retention/communication pain — ask directly before inviting
- Will commit to giving honest feedback (weekly check-in, 15 minutes)

**Beta incentive:** 3 months free after launch (not a discount — free)

---

### Soft Launch: Month 3 — First Paying Customers

**Goal:** 8 paying customers by end of Month 3.

**Week 1-2:**
- Announce in IIABA online communities: "We ran a 30-day beta. Here's what happened. Beacon is now live." Post the number — total letters generated, total clients the beta agents retained.
- Email every beta user: "Beacon is live. Your 3 free months start now. If you know one agent who has this problem, tell them."
- Post on LinkedIn with beta results — use numbers, not adjectives

**Week 3-4:**
- Share one detailed beta agent story (with permission): what they did before Beacon, what they did with it, specific results
- Reach out to 3 IIABA state chapter directors for upcoming meeting slots
- Submit first Insurance Journal article pitch

---

### Growth: Months 4-6 — Build the Flywheel

**Goal:** 30 paying customers by end of Month 6.

**Actions:**
- Deliver 2-3 IIABA chapter presentations
- Launch referral program — email all current customers with their referral link
- Publish first Insurance Journal article
- LinkedIn: 3x/week posting rhythm established
- Begin agency network outreach: identify 5 networks, send intro emails
- Collect NPS from all users who have been paid for 30+ days
- Do 3-5 customer interviews: what made you sign up, what almost stopped you, what would you tell another agent

**Conversion focus:** If trial-to-paid conversion is below 15%, do user interviews to understand why before adding more signups.

---

### Scale: Months 7-12 — Compound Growth

**Goal:** 85 paying customers by end of Month 12.

**Actions:**
- Close first agency network partnership
- Referral program driving 25%+ of new signups
- LinkedIn following established with consistent content
- Second Insurance Journal article
- Attend one IIABA national event if budget permits
- Begin tracking NPS formally — target 50+
- Identify 3 potential strategic partners or acquirers to watch
